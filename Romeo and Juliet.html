ROMEO AND JULIET

Despite the fight between their families, they are truely in love with each other. And secretly meet and marry. But, Juliet's family gets her married to another royal family. When she then pleads for the marriage to be delayed, her mother rejects her. On the night before the wedding, she takes a drug and, when discovered apparently dead, she is laid in the family crypt. The messenger, however, does not reach Romeo and, instead, Romeo learns of Juliet's apparent death from his servant Balthasar.Still believing Juliet to be dead, he drinks the poison. Juliet then awakens and, finding Romeo dead, stabs herself with his dagger. The families are reconciled by their children's deaths and agree to end their violent feud. The play ends with the Prince's elegy for the lovers: "For never was a story of more woe / Than this of Juliet and her Romeo.
